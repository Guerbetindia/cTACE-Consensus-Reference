Ideal candidate Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q1
  • Child–Pugh ≤ B7 | ECOG PS 0–1 | no vascular invasion | no extrahepatic disease (EHD)
  • Tumor burden up to INASL-modified BCLC B2
  • Use ALBI grade alongside Child–Pugh for prognostication (BCLC 2022) — better homogeneity in outcomes
Hard contraindications / poor outcome predictors Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q1
  • >50% liver replaced by tumor | bilirubin >3 mg/dL | AST >5× ULN | LDH >425 IU/L
  • Child C, main PVT, hepatic encephalopathy, severe comorbidities
  • Exception: Child >B7 acceptable only if superselective AND patient is being bridged to transplant
When cTACE replaces curative treatment Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q2
  • Curative options (ablation/resection/LT) not technically feasible — difficult anatomy, unsafe location, failed image guidance, high surgical risk, patient refusal
  • Bridging to LT when waiting time expected to exceed 6 months (reduces dropout 3–13%)
Tumor burden — choose technique by SAT score Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q3

Six-and-Twelve (SAT) preferred over TBS and 7–11 for survival stratification.

Special scenario — Portal Vein Tumor Thrombus (PVTT) Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q4

PVTT is no longer an absolute contraindication. cTACE is safe and effective in selected patients with ECOG 0–1, Child A, no EHD, and segmental/subsegmental PVTT — and even some lobar/main PVTT cases — provided the technique is strictly superselective/ultraselective.

  • Lipiodol accumulation in PVTT predicts response: good uptake → OS 10 mo vs 2.7 mo with poor uptake
Special scenario — Hepatic Vein Tumor Thrombus (HVTT) Evidence: Low Rec: Weak Q4
  • Limited evidence; consider in unresectable cases via MDT. Median OS 6.5 mo–1.6 y with cTACE alone
  • Modified cTACE (sandwich technique with cisplatin infusion) reported in select Asia-Pacific centres
Special scenario — Limited Extrahepatic Disease Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q6
  • Limited EHD = solitary bone/adrenal/soft-tissue met, scattered small lung nodules, or regional nodal disease
  • cTACE + systemic therapy is safe in this group; OS 15.1 mo vs 4.7 mo with systemic alone
  • Heavy EHD burden → exclude from cTACE
Imaging workup Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q7
  • Triple-phase CT or dynamic CEMRI within 30 days (max 60 days) of cTACE
  • Add chest CT (NCCT or CECT) when planning with triple-phase CT to rule out pulmonary mets
  • Prefer gadoxetic acid / gadobenate-enhanced MRI over CT for atypical enhancement patterns or small lesions
Drug selection Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q8
  • First-line agents: doxorubicin, cisplatin, epirubicin — no proven superiority of one over another
  • Cap cumulative doxorubicin at 400–450 mg/m² (risk of CTRCD ~9%)
  • Lower-dose doxorubicin (50 mg) → milder PES without compromising response or OS
  • Liposomal doxorubicin → lower toxicity, improved OS vs plain formulation
  • Pre-cTACE cardiac assessment for: LV dysfunction, age >65, planned cumulative anthracycline >300 mg/m²

Emulsion preparation Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q8
  • Water-in-oil emulsion required: Lipiodol-to-aqueous ratio 2:1, 3:1, or 4:1 (4:1 most stable in vitro)
  • Dissolve drug in non-ionic contrast (≥300 mg I/mL) to stabilize emulsion — not saline
  • Use 3-way stopcock with glass/polycarbonate syringes; ≥20 pumping exchanges to achieve 70–100 µm droplet size
  • Prepare immediately before use; re-homogenize during the session
Lipiodol dosing Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q9
  • Standard 10 mL, do NOT exceed 15 mL per session (>20 mL → liver failure, pulmonary toxicity from hepatovenous shunting)
  • Superselective cTACE for small hypervascular HCC: 2–3 mL/cm of tumor diameter
  • Hypovascular small lesions: 1 mL/cm
Prognostic markers to flag pre-procedure Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q25
  • AFP >1000 ng/mL → poor prognosis regardless of treatment
  • PIVKA-II / DCP elevation, FDG-avid lesions, infiltrative morphology, ill-defined capsule
  • FDG PET tumor-to-liver SUV ratio ≥2 → high malignant potential, poor post-cTACE prognosis
  • CLIP score components: PVT, tumor size, Child–Pugh class, AFP
Cone-beam CT — use whenever available Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q10
  • Improves tumor detection (especially <3 cm and recurrent), feeder identification, on-table Lipiodol confirmation, and identification of additional supply
  • Dual-phase CBCT differentiates HCC (corona enhancement) from arterioportal shunts/pseudo-lesions
  • Obviates need for separate immediate post-cTACE CT scan entirely
Catheter position Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q11
  • Order of preference: ultraselective > superselective > selective > nonselective
  • Use 1.7–2 Fr microcatheters for ultraselective work
  • Pharmaco-angiography adjuncts: nitroglycerin 100–200 mcg IA, verapamil, papaverine for spasm; IV heparin for thrombotic risk
Arterioportal / arteriovenous shunts Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q12
  • Identify on DSA or CBCT; treat with selective embolization using particles, gelfoam, coils/plugs, alcohol, or n-BCA
  • Proceed with cTACE only after successful shunt occlusion/reduction
  • SBRT is a viable alternative when shunts cannot be safely closed
Preventing non-target embolization Evidence: Low Rec: Weak Q13
  • Meticulous angiographic mapping + CBCT + selective catheterization
  • Antireflux double-balloon catheters for tortuous/atretic feeders or post-multiple cTACE anatomy
  • Balloon-occluded TACE (b-TACE) — temporary occlusion proximal to all feeders for pressure-driven embolization
  • Side-hole catheter technique when proximal-origin feeders or unstable catheter position
  • Prophylactically embolize non-target vessels that cannot be excluded from the treatment territory
Hypovascular HCC Evidence: Low Rec: Weak Q15
  • Worse RFS, OS, faster TACE refractoriness, poorer prognosis vs hypervascular HCC
  • Prefer thermal ablation or SIRT over cTACE when feasible
  • If proceeding with cTACE: ultraselective + CBCT-guided only
  • Consider collision tumor (HCC-iCCA) or sarcomatoid HCC differential
Lipiodol injection endpoint Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q14
  • Small tumors: visualisation of adjacent portal vein radicles (or stasis)
  • Large tumors: dense Lipiodol uptake OR maximum administrable dose reached
  • Avoid dense lipiodolization of adjoining non-tumor parenchyma
  • Confirm complete uptake on CBCT against pre-op imaging
Particulate embolization after Lipiodol Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q16
  • Gelfoam (1.0–1.5 mm particles) — preferred when repeat cTACE through same artery anticipated; resorbs in 1–2 weeks
  • PVA / tris-acryl microspheres (100–300 µm) — when repeat through index artery not anticipated
  • Inject slowly to near-stasis

Imaging schedule Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q18
  • Standard: 1, 3, 6, 9, 12 months, then every 6 months thereafter
  • Closer follow-up for high-risk patients: high AFP/PIVKA-II, FDG-avid, infiltrative lesions, incomplete treatment, equivocal findings, new symptoms, rising tumor markers
Modality of choice Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q18
  • Dynamic CEMRI with DWI is the investigation of choice (post-contrast T1W + DWI superior to CECT)
  • Lipiodol does not affect MR signal; CT beam-hardening artifacts can obscure small enhancing tumors
  • Spectral CECT if MRI is contraindicated
Response assessment Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q19
  • Use enhancement-based criteria — NOT size-based RECIST 1.1 or WHO
  • mRECIST = current gold standard for radiologic response in HCC locoregional therapy
  • LI-RADS LR-TR = increasingly adopted, especially MRI-based; lesion-level (viable/non-viable/equivocal/non-evaluable)
  • Both mRECIST and LR-TR are valid; choice is institution/radiologist preference
Retreatment timing Evidence: Moderate Rec: Strong Q20
  • On-demand (based on residual disease on follow-up) preferred over on-schedule — fewer adverse effects, equivalent efficacy
  • Staged multi-session cTACE for large tumors: complete within 2-month interval
  • Shorter intervals are safe in Child A; avoid in Child B (higher adverse effects)
  • Repeat decision must be based on tumor response AND patient tolerance reassessed before each session

UnTACEable progressionMassive liver involvement, significant EHD, main PV/1st-order branch invasion, Child B8+, ECOG >2Stop cTACE; escalate to systemic; MDT review
Rule of thumb: Complete 3 sessions before abandoning cTACE — response rate 46% after 2nd, 58% after 3rd in non-responders.
Combination with locoregional therapies Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q22
  • cTACE + RFA/MWA is safe and superior to cTACE alone for HCC 3–5 cm
  • Mechanism: TACE reduces heat-sink effect and treats peripheral micro-mets; synergistic for tumors >3 cm
  • TACE + EBRT/SBRT improves OS over TACE alone in macrovascular invasion (22.7 vs 13.5 mo)

Bridging & downstaging to liver transplant Evidence: High Rec: Strong Q24
  • cTACE is preferred for bridging when waiting time expected >6 months and Y90 not available
  • Y90 has shown longer time-to-progression than cTACE in keeping patients transplant-eligible (when accessible)
  • Useful for downstaging beyond Milan to within accepted criteria (UCSF, up-to-7, Asian, INASL extended)
  • Bridging most impactful in UNOS T2 patients to reduce dropout
